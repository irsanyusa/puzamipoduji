America’s Got Talent Season 18 contestant Mitch Rossell announced on Instagram this week that he would have to postpone his upcoming show. The new date is all the way in May of next year. Fans shared their concern in the comments.

Mitch Rossell Postpones Show Until Next Year

“Due to unforeseen circumstances, we are postponing the Mitch Rossell show for Sunday November 5th at Jergels to Saturday, May 18th at Crafthouse,” reads an announcement posted to Rossell’s Instagram account on Thursday.

The message goes on to tell fans that their tickets for this Sunday’s show will be honored on the May date. The show was originally supposed to take place at Jergel’s Rhythm Grille in Warrendale, Pennsylvania. The new show will be at Crafthouse Stage & Grille in Pittsburgh.

Rossell didn’t share the reason he had to postpone the show. In his post’s comments, fans expressed their concern, with one person writing, “Hope you and your family are well!!” Another wondered why the show was postponed to a date so far into the future.

Rossell Made it to the Season 18 Semifinals

Rossell auditioned for Season 18 of America’s Got Talent with a performance of an original song called “Son.” The track is about losing his father at a young age. All four judges voted “yes” to sending Rossell forward in the competition.

The singer made it to the Semifinals, where he performed another original song called “All I Need to See.” He was in the Top 3 for the night, but unfortunately he didn’t earn enough votes to send him to the Season 18 Finals.

After his elimination from the show, Rossell signed a record deal with Dreamcatcher Artists. As he wrote in the announcement post, “I am so pumped to have a team of people who truly want the world to know me and my music and they’re excited at what we can accomplish.”
